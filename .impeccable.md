## Design Context

### Users
Technical operators launching, monitoring, and troubleshooting autonomous coding runs. They work in a dense operational console, often switching between multiple live sessions, and need to understand what an agent is doing without leaving the UI.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, engineered, and exact. The interface should feel trustworthy under pressure: a precise instrument rather than a chat toy or marketing surface.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark, sleek, and utilitarian with warm graphite surfaces and a restrained mineral-green live accent. Dense without cramping, with strong typographic hierarchy and almost no ornamental decoration. Avoid neon hacker styling, gradients, glass, card grids, and generic chat bubbles.

### Design Principles
- Preserve reader intent and position above all else; following is a reversible state, never a default assumption.
- Give the agent trace primary visual weight and compress orchestrator activity into legible, low-noise seams.
- Use spacing, typography, and disclosure to create scan paths instead of wrapping every event in a card.
- Keep status honest and actions immediate, interruptible, keyboard-accessible, and clearly focused.
- Make long-running and offscreen activity visible without moving the reader.
